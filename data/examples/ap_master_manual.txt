Invoices are received by email and entered into the ERP by the AP clerk.
The clerk validates required fields (vendor, invoice number, date, amount).
If the invoice matches the PO and goods receipt, it can be approved.
Invoices over $5,000 require director approval.
After approval, payment is scheduled for Net 30 and then paid.
--- END OF SECTION ---
If an invoice does not have a PO number, AP routes it to the department manager.
The manager must confirm the expense and provide an account code.
If the manager rejects the expense, AP notifies the vendor and closes the invoice.
If approved, AP enters the account code and proceeds to schedule payment.
--- END OF SECTION ---
AP performs a 3-way match. If there is a quantity mismatch or price variance above the tolerance,
the invoice is placed on hold and AP contacts the vendor for clarification.
Once corrected documents are received, AP updates the invoice and re-runs matching.
--- END OF SECTION ---
The AP clerk receives the invoice via the central email portal.
The clerk validates the invoice. If the invoice is missing a GL account code, the clerk contacts the department manager.
The manager provides the account code.
Once the account code is updated, the invoice is routed for director approval.
If approved, the invoice is scheduled for payment.
--- END OF SECTION ---
Invoices are entered into the ERP.
The system checks for duplicate invoice numbers.
If a duplicate is detected, the system immediately rejects the invoice and notifies the vendor.
If it is not a duplicate, the AP clerk proceeds to match the invoice against the purchase order.
After a successful match, payment is executed.
